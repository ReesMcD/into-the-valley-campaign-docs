- **Key Plot Lines**
    - The [[realm-of-the-forsaken.lands.the-fallen-land-of-elderen.kingdom-of-ravell|Kingdom of Ravell]] is in an ongoing war with the [[realm-of-the-forsaken.lands.the-fallen-land-of-elderen.kingdom-of-cordach|Kingdom of Cordach]] over border disputes that has lasted since the fall of the empire.
    - Queen Evelyn St. Claire has been killed, and Raven Adrianus, the leader of the mercenary band Band of the Crow, has usurped the throne. The whereabouts of the rightful heir, Princess Isobel St. Claire, are unknown.
        - The Queen hired the Band of the Crow to aid in their war against Cordach.
    - The Syndicate is loosely involved here, but it is said to be the home of one of their retired thieves, Garrik Elderose.
    - Ravell is home to The Wrym’s Spine, a mountain range that is said to house a sleeping Dragon because it resembles a dragon’s back. Kobolds are said to live in a tunnel system in these mountains. They are also known for hot springs that have special healing properties.

The Kingdom of Ravell, one of the Nine Kingdoms of Elderen, has a rich and tumultuous history. Located in the Fallen Land of Elderen, it stands as a testament to the realm's complex past and ongoing struggles.

I. Background

- A. Lesser Kingdom: Ravell is considered one of the lesser kingdoms of Elderen, but its influence in the region remains significant.
- B. Located in The Fallen Land of Elderen: This placement ties Ravell to the broader history of Elderen, including the fall of the ancient elven empire.

II. Ongoing Conflict

- A. War with Kingdom of Cordach: A long-standing border dispute has escalated into open warfare, straining resources and affecting civilian life.
- B. Border disputes since fall of empire: The conflict's roots trace back to the power vacuum left by the collapsed empire, highlighting the kingdom's struggle for stability and power.

III. Recent Political Upheaval

- A. Death of Queen Evelyn St. Claire: The sudden loss of the monarch has thrown the kingdom into turmoil.
- B. Usurpation by Raven Adrianus
    - 1. Leader of Band of the Crow (mercenary group): Adrianus's background as a mercenary leader suggests a significant shift in governance style.
    - 2. Previously hired by Queen to aid in war: This twist of fate underscores the volatile nature of power in Ravell.
- C. Missing heir: Princess Isobel St. Claire, known as the Mithral Maiden, has disappeared, adding mystery and potential for future conflict.

IV. Geography

- A. Capital: Ereford, the heart of Ravell's political and economic life.
- B. The Wrym's Spine mountain range
    - 1. Legend of sleeping dragon: This myth adds a layer of mystique to the kingdom's landscape.
    - 2. Kobold-inhabited tunnel system: The presence of these creatures hints at the diverse and sometimes dangerous fauna of Ravell.
    - 3. Healing hot springs: A natural resource that could be both an economic boon and a strategic asset in times of war.

V. Notable Connections

- A. The Syndicate's involvement: The presence of this secretive organization suggests underlying political intrigues.
- B. Garrik Elderose: A retired Syndicate thief residing in Ravell, potentially serving as a link between the kingdom's present and its shadowy past.
